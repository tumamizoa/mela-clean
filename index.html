What "mela clean" means

The places a normal clean leaves behind.

A surface can look clean from across the room. Mela clean means going back over the bits that get skipped, the grooves, edges and corners where everything else collects.

Tracks & grooves

Window and door tracks, shower screen rails, oven door seals: the channels that trap grit and don't get a second look.

Grout & tile lines

Bathroom and kitchen grout scrubbed back, not just wiped over, where mould and soap scum actually build up.

Skirting boards & edges

The dust line along floors and behind doors that accumulates for months between cleans.

Range hoods & vents

Filters, exhaust covers and air vents that quietly collect grease and dust over time.

Switches, handles & rails

Light switches, door handles, taps and railings: touched constantly, cleaned rarely.

Behind & under

Behind the toilet, under the bed, behind appliances: the spots that only get done when something's moved.

Air conditioning

Filters and vents wiped down and cleared of built-up dust, so the air moving through the room is clean too.

Windows & sills

Glass cleaned streak-free and sills wiped out, including the tracks where dust and dead insects collect.

Plus everything else

Kitchens, bathrooms, floors and surfaces: the full clean, done properly, every time.
